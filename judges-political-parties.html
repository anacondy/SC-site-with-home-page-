HISTORICAL CONTEXT

The relationship between the judiciary and politics in India has been complex. While the Constitution ensures judicial independence, several retired judges have taken on political roles, advisory positions, or party memberships. The most notable case was Justice A.N. Ray, who as a dissenting judge in the Kesavananda Bharati case, was controversially appointed as Chief Justice of India by superseding three senior judges. He later joined the Rajya Sabha as a Congress nominee.

This practice has often sparked debates about the separation of powers and judicial independence. Some argue that post-retirement political appointments may compromise the judiciary's perceived independence, while others view it as a legitimate exercise of democratic rights by retired judges.
